Life on Mars

How far we've come, and how we'll get there.

Kamal Elliott
Since the beginning of the space race, Mars has been a tangible goal for NASA’s exploration efforts. Its potential and proximity leads many to believe that it could be a second home for humanity.
In fact, it has become a request from a certain science-oriented president on his way out of office. President Obama recently wrote an Op-Ed calling for NASA and the U.S. private sector to cooperate in order to colonize the planet.
While NASA has worked closely with various universities and well-known names like Lockheed Martin or Boeing, SpaceX is one of the few firms with Mars as its primary target. The company seeks to launch multiple resupply missions to whoever is on the planet by 2030.
Every successful SpaceX launch brings them closer to that goal, with failure acting as a valuable lesson and NASA’s history as a guide.
